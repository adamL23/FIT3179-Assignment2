Weather In Australia

This visualisation observes the temperature difference and rainfall between different states in Australia. Australia's climate varies between the eight states and territories. There are four seasons across the country and a wet and dry season in the tropical north.

Average Temperature From 2019-2020

Australia is a land of extremes with temperatures ranging from highs of 40°C in the central desert regions to below freezing in the higher regions of the country's southeast. Sometimes these extremes can be experienced on a single day.

The visual map on the right shows the average temperature throughout the eight states in Australia. States in the south are notably colder to those in the north and west.

Norther Territory has shown to be the hottest with the maximum temperature reaching up to 32°C, while Tasmania is the coldest where it reaches it minimum temperature of 8°C.

The line chart below observes the tempearutre change throughout the year for each state.

Rainfall Recorded In Each State.

The following visualisation shows the recorded rainfall amount (per mm) for each state in Australia from 2019-2020.

Rainfall in Australia is highly variable with low average rainfall per year throughout the country and intense seasonal rains in the tropics.

Amount of rainfall varies between each state and the time of season. Queensland have shown to record the highest volume of rainfall among the eight states, notably in the month of January, due to monsoon season that begins from October to May.
